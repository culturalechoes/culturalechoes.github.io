Cultural Echoes

Cultural Echoes is an international, non-governmental and independent organization based in Skopje, Macedonia.

With a background ranging from architecture to computer science, stopping by law and several of the arts, our mission is to promote urban and environmental responsibility. Aiming to rediscover, redefine and address common issues through smart strategies. Opening new perspectives in order to create a more sustainable, socio-culturally friendly and modern urban environment.

Our Team

Simona Dimkovska

President

Simona Dimkovska was born in 1989 in Kumanovo. Show More

She graduates at "Goce Delchev" high school in 2008 and receives her bachelor degree in architecture at the Faculty for Architecture within the "Sts. Cyril and Methodius" University in Skopje, in 2011. Attending the same university, she gains her bachelor degree in acting at the Faculty for Dramatic Arts in 2015.

She successfully combines and applies knowledge from both areas. She works as a set designer for the plays Ljubof (2013) – produced at the Youth Cultural Center in Skopje, as well as Carnage (2014), a play of a master studies student, produces at the Faculty for Dramatic Arts.

As an actress, she works in the plays of the well-established Slobodan Unkovski: Solun – grad na duhovi (2014/2015), Lerin (2016) – both produced at the independent "Navigator's Cvetko Theater." She performs in Einstein's dreams (2015), co-produced with the National theater "Vojdan Chernodrinski" in Prilep. In 2016 she performs in the play Antigone and Creon, directed by Nela Vitosevic. In the field of film and television she stars in few TV advertisements, one of them directed by Milcho Manchevski. She also appears in a TV series show, directed by Timur Alpatov, where she acts a character in Russian. She has a role in the movie The secret ingredient (2017), directed by Gjorce Stavreski.

She has participated in many workshops revolving around different topics: Chekhov, voice and body, theater and politics etc. She speaks English and Serbian.

Tomás Romero Talley

Vice-President

Tomás Romero Talley, Spanish-U.S. citizen born in Barcelona, 1994. Show More

Has lived between the States and Barcelona, where he is currently based. Grew up as part of the Fab Lab Kids' pilot program at the Fab Lab Barcelona – an initiative of MIT's Center for Bits and Atoms, lead by physicist Neil Gershenfeld. In 2017, enrolled with a scholarship in the 'Global Summer School' at the Institute of Advanced Architecture of Catalonia (IAAC).

Currently completing a Bachelor's Degree in Architecture at the Polytechnic University of Catalonia.

Like many humans in their twenties, has held a broad range of multidisciplinary jobs: Construction Apprentice 2014; Draftsman in AART Architectural Firm 2015; Intern Collaborator at Fab Lab Barcelona 2015; Security Guard 2018; Start-Up co-founder in Barcelona 2018.
